Hive

INTRODUCTION

  • CLIENT

    • CareerFoundry (Case Study). Hive was created as part of my UI Design course.

  • OBJECTIVE

    • Hive helps to connect the neighbourhood with skills and services offered locally. The user will be able to search for helpers that can get that TV ﬁnally mounted on the wall, help an elderly person with everyday errands, walk the dog, ﬁnd a baby sitter, etc.

  • THE CHALLENGE

    • There are apps out there that offer a similar idea in Canada, but they tend to offer limited services, for example AskForTask, they do not offer small jobs like tutoring. I myself offer computer tutoring for seniors locally but when I approached them to sign as a tasker, they said unfortunately they do not have a category for tutoring/teaching. A successful app called TaskRabbit in the US offers similar context which is more widely used. Hive will concentrate on local neighbourhoods and offer all the small and large jobs around the home (not limited to) with a large variety of categories to pick from. A simple to use interface that can be accessed by the average Jo!

  • USERS

    • Anyone who is seeking help locally and anyone who has a service to provide to their neighbourhood.

  • DURATION

    • 6 Weeks

  • PLATFORM

    • Android and iOS

  • TOOLS
    • Sketch
    • InVision

    • Usability Hub

DESIGN DECISIONS

A challenging task was the design of the home page. I wanted to keep to the theme of the honeycomb and to distribute the categories accordingly. Originally, I wanted to include pictures of humans to make the app more "human-like", but soon was faced with how to incorporate the text as well in a small space on a mobile device. After some trials I opted for the removal of the pictures and replaced them with icons that resemble these categories which look more contemporary as well as functional. The user is the most important factor in this equation therefore these two images went out to 25 users as a user preference test. Results were 99% in favour of the second design therefore it is the winner as the home page design for Hive.
