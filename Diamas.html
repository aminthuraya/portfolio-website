Diamas

INTRODUCTION

  • CLIENT

    • CareerFoundry (Case Study). Diamas was created as part of my UI Design course.

  • OBJECTIVE

    • CareerFoundry envisioned to make the process easier for consumers who are interested in browsing and buying beautiful glamorous jewelry to match their particular needs. I wanted to create a simple, easy to use, aesthetically pleasing user experience with secure uncomplicated payment process.

  • THE CHALLENGE

    • There are many e-commerce websites that offer a jewelry shopping experience. I have not been able to see many great mobile apps out there that offer a clean pleasing UI experience with a simple checkout process.

  • USERS

    • Anyone who is seeking to find beautiful jewelry, purchase online safely and with ease, aimed at both Male and Female, for a memorable occasion or a gift for someone special.

  • DURATION

    • 6 Weeks

  • PLATFORM

    • Android

  • TOOLS

    • InVision
    • OmniGraffle
    • Adobe Suite

STYLE GUIDE

Creating a brand for my app was important as this is how I want the app to be perceived in my user’s mind. It sets the feel for the app, how I want the user to feel every time they see anything related to Diamas. I want the first thing that comes to my user’s mind when they hear of Diamas is “Luxury”. Below is a Brand Guidelines documents which defines the brand elements like the logo, color, typography, iconography and the writing style.


USER FLOW DIAGRAM

After defining my MVP and a task analysis for each of my user stories, I compiled these user stories in one user flow diagram to define the entry point and the path a user would take to reach their goal.


WIREFRAMES

Low, mid and high-fidelity wireframes were created to visualize my design by first sketching with pen and paper and then refined the wireframes in Sketch. Wireframes are the communication tool between the client and designer and the rest of the team. In this case the high-fidelity stage was shared with 10 users for constructive feedback which was very welcomed, iterations and adjustments were made before diving into the final design.

RETROSPECTIVE

Feedback has been very encouraging for this app. I feel I have achieved the goals set out in the brief. The app has an elegant and expensive feel and very happy with the colour scheme and choice of font to complement the design. I still feel the app could benefit from more elements, for example adding videos of the products which show a 360 degree angle of a ring, this will further entice the customer to buy the item. Mixed opinions from test users that maybe an icon added next to each category on the home page, this option could be further explored in the future. Credits go out to all my test subjects for their constructive and valuable feedback as well as my tutor and mentor throughout this project.
